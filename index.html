College Sports Is Being Sold

The fans who
built it weren't
invited.

Conference realignment. Private equity buyouts. Every major decision in college sports is being made without the people who fill the stadiums, fund the programs, and bleed their school's colors for life.

And when money drives the decisions, football and basketball get protected. Wrestling gets cut. Swimming gets cut. Gymnastics gets cut. The fans of those programs — some of the most passionate people in college athletics — have the least voice of anyone.

The Fans' Network is changing that. Every fan. Every sport. Every school. Claim your seat and we'll show you exactly how.

01
The Rooms You Were Never In

PE-backed companies now control multimedia rights at hundreds of programs. Conference commissioners signed billion-dollar TV deals. You found out when everyone else did — when it was already done.

02
Olympic Sports Pay the Price

When money drives decisions, revenue sports get protected and everything else gets reviewed on a spreadsheet. Wrestling. Swimming. Gymnastics. Women's soccer. The fans of those programs are the most overlooked people in college athletics.

03
Organized Fans Change the Math

Individual fans — no matter how loud — don't move the needle. Organized fans across every sport, unified under one school, are a constituency. That's what we're building, school by school.
